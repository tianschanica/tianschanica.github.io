The Exhibition

An introduction to the range and its significance

"Tian Shan" — 天山 — the Heavenly Mountains. A 2,500-kilometer arc of ice and stone spanning the heart of Central Asia, where the ancient Silk Road wove between glaciated summits that rise to altitudes where air thins and the world below disappears.

This exhibition documents a 92-day expedition through the Tian Shan range, recording the geological, ecological, and human dimensions of one of Earth's most spectacular yet least-visited mountain systems. From the Zailiysky Alatau above Almaty to the glaciated heights of Khan Tengri on the Kazakhstan–Kyrgyzstan–China tri-border, the journey traversed terrain unchanged since caravans of the Silk Road passed below.

The range shelters snow leopards and Marco Polo sheep, ancient Tian Shan spruce forests, and nomadic traditions stretching back centuries — all under the looming permanence of 7,000-meter summits cloaked in eternal ice.

"The mountains are not stadiums where I satisfy my ambition to achieve, they are cathedrals where I practice my religion." — Anatoli Boukreev

07

Expedition Journal

Key milestones across 92 days in the field

Jun 1
Departure — Almaty, Kazakhstan

The expedition departs Almaty at dawn. First sight of the Zailiysky Alatau range: snow-capped summits emerging from morning haze above the city at 847 m.

847 m
Jun 8
Issyk-Kul Traverse

Circumnavigation of Lake Issyk-Kul, Central Asia's largest alpine lake. Water temperature 17 °C despite surrounding snow peaks. Edelweiss fields in full bloom along the southern shore.

1,607 m
Jun 22
Inylchek Glacier — First Crossing

The South Inylchek glacier stretches 60 km — one of the longest outside polar regions. Navigating the chaotic icefall required 3 days. Crevasse depths measured at 40+ meters.

3,800 – 4,200 m
Jul 3
Base Camp Established — Khan Tengri

The marble peak of Khan Tengri reveals itself — a perfect pyramid of white marble rising 7,010 m. Named "Ruler of the Spirits" in Kazakh. Not climbed until 1931.

4,100 m
Jul 18
SUMMIT — Khan Tengri, 7,010 m

Summit achieved at 06:42 AM after a 14-hour push from high camp. Temperature: −42 °C, wind 80 km/h. Visibility: 400 km in all directions. The peak's shadow stretched to the horizon at sunrise.

7,010 m ▲
Aug 5
Border Crossing — China (Xinjiang)

Entry to China via the Torugart Pass. Landscape transforms: alpine meadows give way to the Tarim Basin. Ancient Silk Road caravanserai visible in the valley below.

3,752 m
Aug 24
Turfan Depression

Descent to the Turfan Depression at −154 m below sea level — the world's second lowest point. Temperature: +49 °C. The contrast with glaciated summits 5 days prior is staggering.

−154 m
Sep 1
Return via Silk Road

Final traverse westward through Kashgar and Osh. The Fergana Valley seen in autumn colours from the Kara-Darya valley rim — the expedition concludes at the point where the mountains meet the steppe.

750 m
